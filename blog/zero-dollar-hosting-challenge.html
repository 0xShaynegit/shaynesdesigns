The Zero-Dollar Hosting Challenge: Cut a Client's Tech Bill by 100%

A local contractor saved $888 per year by ditching WordPress. Here's how we helped them migrate to a lightning-fast, unhackable, $0/month website.

Shayne Shayne's Designs
20 April 2026 Published
8 min read Reading time

The Problem with the "WordPress Tax"

Most small businesses are paying a hidden fee for websites that don't work. You are told that a $50 or $60 monthly fee for "managed WordPress hosting" is just the cost of doing business. You are told it covers updates, security, and "peace of mind."

But let's look at the math. If you are paying $60 a month, that is $720 a year. Over five years, that is $3,600. And that is just for the privilege of having your website exist on the internet. It doesn't include the time you spend fixing broken plugins, the cost of premium SEO tools, or the business you lose because your site takes five seconds to load on a customer's phone.

This Case Study Shows
  • Real migration: WordPress to static HTML in one project
  • Real costs: Dropped from $888 to $0 annually
  • Real results: 6.5x faster load times and zero security breaches
  • Real ROI: Migration paid for itself in less than one year

At Shayne's Designs, we believe that money belongs back in your marketing budget. We set out to prove that you can have a world-class, professional website without a monthly subscription fee. We call it the Zero-Dollar Hosting Challenge.

The Client: A Local Hero with a Global Headache

Our client is a local residential contractor. Their WordPress site was "fine," but it felt sluggish. Every time they wanted to change a photo or update their pricing, they were terrified of breaking something. The worst part? They were paying nearly $900 a year for a site that was hurting their Google rankings because it was too slow.

The Before Numbers

Monthly Hosting: $59.00 (Managed WordPress Hosting)
Premium Plugins: $15.00/month (Security and Backup tools)
Total Yearly Cost: $888.00
Google PageSpeed Score: 42/100 (Mobile)
Security: Had been hacked twice in three years due to outdated plugins.

They were stuck in the WordPress loop. Paying for tools to fix problems that only exist because they were using WordPress.

The Shayne's Designs Solution

We didn't just "tweak" their site. We performed a total digital transplant. We took the heart of their business—their content, their photos, and their brand—and moved it onto a modern, high-performance stack.

1. Ditching the Database

WordPress needs a database to work. Every time a customer visits, the server has to "talk" to the database to figure out what to show. This is slow and vulnerable. We rebuilt the site using static HTML. No database means there is nothing to slow down and nothing for a hacker to break into.

2. The Power of GitHub and Cloudflare

We moved the site's files to GitHub and connected them to Cloudflare Pages. In 2026, these are the tools used by massive tech companies, but they offer incredible free tiers for small businesses.

GitHub stores the code and acts as a "Time Machine." If we ever make a mistake, we can undo it in one click. Cloudflare delivers the site to customers from servers all over the world. The result? The hosting cost dropped from $59.00 a month to $0.00.

3. The Claude AI Brain

The client was worried they wouldn't be able to edit the site without the WordPress dashboard. We introduced them to a custom Claude Project. Instead of logging into a clunky backend, they just send a message. "Hey Claude, we just finished a job in the West End. Here are three photos and a quick blurb. Can you add this to our portfolio page?"

Shayne's Designs manages the technical side. The client manages the content through simple conversation.

The Payback Period

The one-time migration fee for Shayne's Designs was roughly equal to what the client was going to spend on hosting and plugins over the next 12 months. By month 13, the website was officially "profit-positive." Every dollar they would have spent on the "WordPress Tax" is now going into Google Ads and local community sponsorships.

Why the Site Got Faster

In 2026, Google rewards Core Web Vitals. These are specific measurements of how fast your site feels to a human being. WordPress is "bloated." It loads hundreds of lines of code that your site doesn't actually need just so it can support "themes" and "plugins."

Shayne's Designs writes clean, semantic code. When a customer clicks your link, their phone only has to download the exact text and images they are looking at. Because the site is hosted on Cloudflare's global network, the files are sitting on a server physically close to your customer. Whether they are in the client's town or the next state over, the site feels like an app on their phone. It is instant.

Security: The Peace of Mind That is Actually Free

Our client was tired of getting emails about "Critical Security Updates" for WordPress. They felt like they were constantly looking over their shoulder.

With the Shayne's Designs setup, that stress is gone. Static sites are like a digital "Fort Knox." Because there is no "login" page and no "database," there is no door for a hacker to kick in. You don't need to pay for security plugins because the architecture itself is secure. This is the ultimate "set it and forget it" solution.
