The Three Pieces

A working website has three separate components. Here's what each does and who manages it:

🌐 Domain Name
example.com — Your website address. You own this. You pay $10–$15/year to a registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains).
🖥️ Hosting
Vercel — The server that stores your website files and serves them to visitors. Included with your project cost for year 1. After that: $20–$50/month or free (depending on traffic).
✉️ Email
you@example.com — Your professional email address. Separate service (Google Workspace, Mailgun, etc.). $5–$6/month for business email.

These are separate services. Your domain registrar, hosting provider, and email provider don't have to be the same company (though they can be).

Domain Names Explained

What is it?

Your domain name is your website address. example.com is a domain. Visitors type it in the browser to reach your site.

Who owns it?

You do. You register it and renew it each year. The registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains) keeps records of ownership, but you control it.

How much does it cost?

$10–$15 per year, depending on the extension (.com, .co, .io, etc.) and registrar. Some extensions cost more ($40+/year for premium ones).

Where do I register a domain?

Connecting Your Domain to Hosting

After you buy your domain, you need to point it to your hosting server (Vercel). We do this by:

  1. Getting your nameservers from Vercel
  2. Entering them into your domain registrar
  3. Waiting 24–48 hours for DNS to propagate
  4. Your domain now points to your site

We handle this for you at launch. No action needed on your part.

Hosting is the server that stores your website files and serves them to visitors. When someone visits your domain, they're actually requesting files from the hosting server.

Email is separate from your website. you@example.com is your professional email. It runs on an email server, not your website host.

Common Questions

Can I move my domain to a different host?

Yes. You own your domain. It's not tied to any host. You can point it at any server you want by updating your DNS nameservers. It takes 24–48 hours to take effect.

What if I don't want to pay for hosting after Year 1?

After Year 1, hosting isn't mandatory. Your domain still works, but you'd need to move it somewhere else. We can help with the migration ($60/hour), or you can leave it offline.

Can I use Gmail for my business email?

Gmail personally, yes. But for professional you@yourdomain.com email, you need Google Workspace ($6/month). Regular Gmail only supports @gmail.com addresses.

Is my email secure?

Yes, if you use a reputable provider (Google Workspace, Zoho, Mailgun). They all use encryption, spam filtering, and security protocols. Use a strong password and enable 2FA.

What if my domain registrar goes out of business?

Your domain is yours. You can always transfer it to another registrar. ICANN (the domain authority) enforces this. No single company can hold your domain hostage.

Can you help with any of this?

Yes. We can help set up domains, connect them to hosting, and configure email forwarding. Our optional $300/month support includes ongoing help with these services. Or hire us hourly at $60/hour.
