WordPress vs. Static HTML: Which One Actually Helps You Grow?

WordPress is heavy and expensive. Static HTML is fast, secure, and costs almost nothing. Learn why the modern web is built on simplicity.

20 April 2026 Published
7 min read Reading time

TL;DR

WordPress is like a heavy, gas-guzzling SUV. It has lots of features, but it's expensive to keep running and breaks down often. A static HTML site on GitHub is like a high-end electric sports car. It's lightning-fast, incredibly safe, and costs almost nothing to "fuel." The Great Debate: Heavy vs. High-Performance

Most people use WordPress because they think they have to. It's been around forever, and everyone knows the name. But in 2026, the internet has changed. Speed and security are now the most important factors for ranking on Google and keeping customers happy.

Here is the honest breakdown of how the old way (WordPress) compares to the modern way (Static HTML on GitHub).

WordPress: The "Standard" Choice

The Good: It is very flexible. If you want to add a store, a membership area, or a complex forum, there is a "plugin" for that.

The Bad: Every time you add a plugin, your site gets slower. WordPress sites are "dynamic," meaning a computer has to build your page every time someone clicks on it. This makes it a prime target for hackers and a nightmare for loading speeds.

The Cost: You are looking at $300 to $1,000+ a year in hosting, security, and "maintenance" just to make sure the site doesn't break when a plugin updates.

Static HTML on GitHub: The "Modern" Choice

The Good: It is incredibly fast. Because the pages are already "built" and sitting on a server (GitHub/Cloudflare), they load the instant a customer clicks. It is also virtually unhackable because there is no database for hackers to break into.

The Bad: It is harder for a regular person to edit. You can't just log into a "dashboard" and click a button to change a sentence. You usually need to know a little bit of code. Unless you have a partner like Shayne's Designs.

The Cost: Hosting is often free. Your only real cost is your domain name and a small fee for expert updates.

Strengths and Weaknesses at a Glance

Feature WordPress Static HTML (GitHub)
Speed Often slow and bloated Instant global loading
Security High risk (needs constant patches) Fort Knox (no database to hack)
Monthly Fees High (Hosting + Plugins) Zero to Near-Zero
Updates Visual Editor (but can break site) Needs Code (or AI Partner)
SEO Good (if you pay for plugins) Best (Speed is a top ranking factor)
