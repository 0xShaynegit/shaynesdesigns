What to Include

To create a strong case study, gather this information from your client work:

  • Starting Problem: What was the client's pain point? (slow site, outdated platform, high costs)
  • Business Goal: What outcome mattered most? (conversions, leads, reduced overhead)
  • What Changed: Technical architecture, design, content restructure, etc.
  • Real Metrics: Performance (load time before/after), Lighthouse score, Core Web Vitals
  • Measurement Context: Test date, device, network condition (e.g., "3G on iPhone 12, 21 July 2026")
  • Business Outcome: If measurable, mention it (but be honest: "Client reported 15% increase in lead submissions after 60 days" is better than inventing %)
  • Client Quote: Real testimonial about the process and result
  • Live Link: Where can readers see the work?

Best Practices

  • Be Honest: Avoid overstatement. "Typically 80+ Lighthouse" beats "Perfect 100%".
  • Use Real Numbers: "2.3 seconds on 4G" is more credible than "fast".
  • Get Permission: Always ask clients before featuring their work as a case study.
  • Show Before/After: Screenshots or performance graphs make the case concrete.
  • Explain the Why: Readers want to know why you made decisions, not just what you built.

Gathering Metrics

Tools to collect real performance data:

  • Lighthouse: Chrome DevTools → Lighthouse. Use the same device/network for before/after.
  • WebPageTest: webpagetest.org — detailed performance waterfalls and comparisons.
  • Core Web Vitals: PageSpeed Insights or Google Analytics for real user data.
  • Screenshots: Before/after design comparisons make impact visual.
